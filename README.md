**Ashton Dumar Norwill Simmonds** (born April 5, 1995), known professionally as **Daniel Caesar**, is a Canadian singer and songwriter. After independently building a following through the release of two [EPs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extended_play), *Praise Break* (2014) and *Pilgrim's Paradise* (2015), Caesar released his debut studio album, *[Freudian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freudian_(album))*, in 2017, which received three [Grammy Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammy_Award) nominations, winning for the collaboration with American singer [H.E.R.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H.E.R.) "[Best Part](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Best_Part_(song))". In 2019 he released his second studio album, *[Case Study 01](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Case_Study_01)*, longlisted for the [2020 Polaris Music Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Polaris_Music_Prize). In 2021 Caesar featured alongside [Giveon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giveon) on [Justin Bieber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justin_Bieber)'s single "[Peaches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peaches_(Justin_Bieber_song))", his first number-one song on the US [*Billboard* Hot 100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100), and receiving four nominations at the [64th Annual Grammy Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/64th_Annual_Grammy_Awards).

In 2023 Caesar published his third studio album, *[Never Enough](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Never_Enough_(Daniel_Caesar_album))* through [Republic Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_Records), winning his second Juno Award. In 2025, Caesar released his fourth studio album *[Son of Spergy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Son_of_Spergy)*, which became his third top-ten album on the [*Billboard* Canadian Albums](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_Canadian_Albums) and first on the [*Billboard* 200](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_200).

Through his career, Caesar has collaborated with several artists, including [Brandy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brandy_Norwood), [Kali Uchis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kali_Uchis), [Ty Dolla Sign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign), [Summer Walker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Summer_Walker), [Common](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_(rapper)), [Omar Apollo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omar_Apollo), [Bon Iver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bon_Iver), and has written songs for [Mary J. Blige](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mary_J._Blige), [SZA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SZA), [Tyler, the Creator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator), [Shawn Mendes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shawn_Mendes) and [Kanye West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kanye_West). He was also awarded with a total of four [Juno Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juno_Awards), two [Soul Train Music Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soul_Train_Music_Awards), a [MTV Video Music Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards) and an [ASCAP Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASCAP_Awards).

**Kendrick Lamar Duckworth** (born June 17, 1987) is an American rapper, songwriter, and record producer. Rooted in [West Coast hip-hop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Coast_hip-hop), Lamar's music features [conscious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conscious_rap), introspective lyrics, with [political criticism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_criticism) and [social commentary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_commentary) concerning [African-American culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African-American_culture). Journalists have referred to Lamar as one of the greatest rappers of all time. In 2018, he became the first musician outside of the [classical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_music) and [jazz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jazz) genres to receive the [Pulitzer Prize for Music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize_for_Music).

Born and raised in [Compton, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compton,_California), Lamar began releasing music under the stage name **K.Dot** in [high school](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secondary_school). He signed with [Top Dawg Entertainment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Top_Dawg_Entertainment) (TDE) in 2005 and co-founded the hip hop [supergroup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supergroup_(music)) [Black Hippy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Hippy). His [alternative rap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternative_hip_hop) debut album, *[Section.80](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Section.80)*, led to a joint contract with [Dr. Dre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dr._Dre)'s [Aftermath Entertainment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aftermath_Entertainment) and [Interscope Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interscope_Records). He rose to stardom with his [gangsta rap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gangsta_rap)–influenced second album *[Good Kid, M.A.A.D City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Good_Kid,_M.A.A.D_City)* (2012), which became the [longest-charting hip hop studio album](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_200#Most_weeks_on_the_chart) on the [*Billboard* 200](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_200) and was named the greatest [concept album](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concept_album) of all time by *[Rolling Stone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_Stone)*. In 2015, he had his first [*Billboard* Hot 100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100) number-one single, with the remix of [Taylor Swift](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taylor_Swift)'s "[Bad Blood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bad_Blood_(Taylor_Swift_song))", and released his first of five consecutive number-one albums on the *Billboard 200*, *[To Pimp a Butterfly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/To_Pimp_a_Butterfly)*, which infused hip-hop with historical [African-American music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African-American_music) genres such as jazz, [funk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funk), and [soul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soul_music).

Lamar's critical and commercial success continued with his [R&B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B) and [pop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pop_music)-leaning fourth album *[Damn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damn_(album))* (2017), featuring his second US number-one single, "[Humble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humble_(song))". He contributed to [the soundtrack](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Panther:_The_Album) of the 2018 film *[Black Panther](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Panther_(film))*, earning a nomination for the [Academy Award for Best Original Song](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Song) for "[All the Stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/All_the_Stars)". His fifth album, *[Mr. Morale & the Big Steppers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mr._Morale_%26_the_Big_Steppers)* (2022), delved into introspection and concluded his tenure with TDE and Aftermath. In 2024, his [highly publicized feud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drake%E2%80%93Kendrick_Lamar_feud) with [Drake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drake_(musician)) and sixth album *[GNX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNX_(album))* spawned the *Billboard* Hot 100 number-one singles "[Like That](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Like_That_(Future,_Metro_Boomin_and_Kendrick_Lamar_song))", "[Not Like Us](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Not_Like_Us)", "[Squabble Up](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Squabble_Up)", and "[Luther](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luther_(Kendrick_Lamar_song))". "Not Like Us" won five [Grammy Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammy_Awards), including [Song of the Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Song_of_the_Year) and [Record of the Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Record_of_the_Year), while "Luther" also won the latter and became his longest-charting number-one song in the US, at 13 weeks.

[Lamar's accolades](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_received_by_Kendrick_Lamar) include 27 Grammy Awards—the [most for a rapper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammy_Award_milestones#Most_Grammys_won_by_a_rapper)—two [Primetime Emmy Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Awards), a [Brit Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brit_Awards), 5 [American Music Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Music_Awards), 7 [*Billboard* Music Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_Music_Awards), 11 [MTV Video Music Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards) and a record 37 [BET Hip Hop Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BET_Hip_Hop_Awards). *[Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_(magazine))* listed him as one of the [100 most influential people in the world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_100) in 2016. Three of his works were included in *[Rolling Stone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_Stone)*'s 2020 revision of the "[500 Greatest Albums of All Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_Stone%27s_500_Greatest_Albums_of_All_Time)". In 2025, he headlined the most-watched [Super Bowl halftime show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LIX_halftime_show) in history, while his [Grand National Tour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_National_Tour) with [SZA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SZA) became the highest-grossing co-headlining tour of all time. Outside of music, Lamar co-founded the creative company [PGLang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PGLang) and ventured into film with his longtime creative partner, [Dave Free](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dave_Free).

**Christopher Brent Wood** (born September 19, 1995), known professionally as **Brent Faiyaz**, is an American singer, songwriter, rapper, and record producer. Faiyaz rose to prominence with his guest performance alongside [Shy Glizzy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shy_Glizzy) on [GoldLink](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GoldLink)'s 2016 single "[Crew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crew_(song))". The song peaked at number 45 on the [*Billboard* Hot 100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100), received [octuple platinum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_recording_certification) certification by the [Recording Industry Association of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America) (RIAA), and was nominated for [Best Rap/Sung Performance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Best_Rap/Sung_Performance) at the [59th Annual Grammy Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/59th_Annual_Grammy_Awards). The following year, Faiyaz released his debut studio album, *[Sonder Son](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonder_Son)* (2017). He is noted for his "sly vocals" and "atmospheric [slow jam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slow_jam) productions," along with occasional rapping.

He first embarked on his musical career in 2013 with the release of his debut extended play (EP), *Black Child* followed by his second, *A.M. Paradox* (2016). In 2016, Faiyaz formed the R&B trio Sonder, for which he serves as lead vocalist. In February 2020, Faiyaz released his fourth EP, *[Fuck the World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuck_the_World_(EP))*, which marked his first entry on the [*Billboard* 200](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_200), peaking at number 20. His second studio album, *[Wasteland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wasteland_(Brent_Faiyaz_album))* (2022) peaked at number two on the *Billboard* 200 and spawned the *Billboard* Hot 100-charting singles, "[Gravity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gravity_(Brent_Faiyaz_and_DJ_Dahi_song))" (with [DJ Dahi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DJ_Dahi) featuring [Tyler, the Creator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyler,_the_Creator)), "[Wasting Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wasting_Time_(Brent_Faiyaz_song))" (featuring [Drake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drake_(musician)) and [the Neptunes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Neptunes)), and "[Price of Fame](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Price_of_Fame_(Brent_Faiyaz_song))", as well as the [TikTok](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TikTok) viral single "[Dead Man Walking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dead_Man_Walking_(Brent_Faiyaz_song))". His debut mixtape, *[Larger than Life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Larger_than_Life_(mixtape))* (2023), debuted at number 11 on the *Billboard* 200 and was met with continued critical praise.

Along with a Grammy Award nomination, his other nominations include [Favorite Male R&B Artist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Music_Award_for_Favorite_Soul/R%26B_Male_Artist) at the [American Music Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Music_Awards) in 2022 and [Best Male R&B/Pop Artist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BET_Award_for_Best_Male_R%26B/Pop_Artist) at the [BET Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BET_Awards) in 2023 and 2024. Faiyaz founded the independent creative agency and record label [ISO Supremacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO_Supremacy) in 2023, which has signed fellow [DMV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_metropolitan_area) native, [Tommy Richman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tommy_Richman).

**Giveon Dezmann Evans** ([US](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_English): [/ˈɡɪviɑːn/](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English); born February 21, 1995), [mononymously](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mononym) known as **Giveon** (stylized as **GIVĒON**), is an American [R&B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B) singer and songwriter. He rose to prominence with his 2019 song "Like I Want You", which peaked at number 87 on the [*Billboard* Hot 100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100). In 2020, Giveon signed with [Epic Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epic_Records) to release two extended plays: the [Grammy Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammy_Award)-nominated *[Take Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Take_Time_(Giveon_EP))* and *[When It's All Said and Done](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/When_It%27s_All_Said_and_Done)*; the former was preceded by the single "[Heartbreak Anniversary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heartbreak_Anniversary)", which peaked at number 16 on the chart and received [quintuple platinum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_recording_certification) certification by the [Recording Industry Association of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America) (RIAA).

Evans guest performed alongside [Daniel Caesar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Caesar) on [Justin Bieber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justin_Bieber)'s 2021 hit single "[Peaches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peaches_(Justin_Bieber_song))", which debuted atop the [*Billboard* Global 200](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_Global_200), *Billboard* Hot 100, and [Canadian Hot 100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100), and received four [Grammy Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grammy_Award) nominations. That same year, his two EPs were bundled into the compilation album *When It's All Said and Done*.... *Take Time* (2021), which peaked at number five on the [*Billboard* 200](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_200). In 2021, he guest performed alongside [Lil Durk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lil_Durk) on Drake's song "[In the Bible](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In_the_Bible)" which peaked at number 7 on the *Billboard* Hot 100, becoming his second top ten song on the chart. His debut studio album, *[Give or Take](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Give_or_Take)* (2022), peaked at number 11 on the *Billboard* 200, while his second studio album, *[Beloved](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beloved_(Giveon_album))* (2025), peaked at number 8.

**Daystar Shemuel Shua Peterson** (born July 27, 1992), known professionally as **Tory Lanez**, is a Canadian singer and rapper. Known for his versatility and blending [R&B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B) and [hip-hop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip-hop) in his music, he was first discovered by [Sean Kingston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sean_Kingston). He came to initial recognition with his mixtape *Conflicts of My Soul: The 416 Story*, released in August 2013. In 2015, Lanez signed with record producer [Benny Blanco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benny_Blanco)'s [Mad Love Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benny_Blanco#Record_labels), an imprint of [Interscope Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interscope_Records).

Lanez's debut studio album, *[I Told You](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I_Told_You)* (2016) explored [alternative R&B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternative_R%26B) and was commercially successful. Its two lead singles, "[Say It](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Say_It_(Tory_Lanez_song))" and "[Luv](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luv_(Tory_Lanez_song))", peaked at numbers 23 and 19 on the US [*Billboard* Hot 100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100), respectively. Both released in 2018, Lanez's second and third albums, *[Memories Don't Die](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memories_Don%27t_Die)* and *[Love Me Now?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Love_Me_Now%3F)* delved further into [hip-hop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip-hop) and were met with continued commercial success despite mixed reviews; the former became his first album to peak atop the [Canadian Albums Chart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canadian_Albums_Chart), while the latter spawned the [double platinum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RIAA_certification)-certified single "[Talk to Me](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Talk_to_Me_(Tory_Lanez_and_Rich_the_Kid_song))" (with [Rich the Kid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rich_the_Kid)). His fourth album and final [major label](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_label) release, *[Chixtape 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chixtape_5)* (2019) peaked at number two on the US [*Billboard* 200](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billboard_200), while his fifth album, *[Daystar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daystar_(album))* (2020), peaked at number ten. He then released his [1980s](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1980s_in_music)-inspired sixth album, *[Alone at Prom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alone_at_Prom)* (2021), followed by his seventh, *[Sorry 4 What](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sorry_4_What)* (2022) until his incarceration. His eighth album, *[Peterson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peterson_(album))* (2025), entered the *Billboard* 200 at number 25.

On August 8, 2023, Lanez was sentenced to ten years in prison after being convicted of shooting [Megan Thee Stallion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megan_Thee_Stallion) in 2020. He was found guilty of first-degree assault with a firearm, discharge of a firearm with gross negligence, and having a concealed firearm in a vehicle.

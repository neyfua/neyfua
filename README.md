- **Ashton Dumar Norwill Simmonds** (born April 5, 1995), known professionally as **Daniel Caesar**, is a Canadian singer and songwriter. After independently building a following through the release of two EPs, *Praise Break* (2014) and *[Pilgrim's Paradise](https://open.spotify.com/album/2oRaqvWFLOKFUDIt5EEQfa?si=f33d4ed6e74746c5)* (2015), Caesar released his debut studio album, *[Freudian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freudian_(album))*, in 2017, which received three Grammy Award nominations. He released his second studio album, *[Case Study 01](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Case_Study_01)*, in 2019. Caesar featured alongside [Giveon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giveon) on [Justin Bieber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justin_Bieber)'s 2021 single "[Peaches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peaches_(Justin_Bieber_song))", his first number-one song on the US Billboard Hot 100. Caesar's third studio album, *[Never Enough](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Never_Enough_(Daniel_Caesar_album))* (2023), is his first release under Republic Records. In 2025, Caesar released his fourth studio album *[Son of Spergy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Son_of_Spergy)*, inspired by reconcilliation with his parents.

- **Giveon Dezmann Evans** (born February 21, 1995), mononymously known as **Giveon** (stylized as **GIVÄ’ON**), is an American [R&B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B) singer and songwriter. He rose to prominence with his 2019 song "Like I Want You", which peaked at number 87 on the Billboard Hot 100. In 2020, Giveon signed with [Epic Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epic_Records) to release two extended plays: the Grammy Award-nominated *[Take Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Take_Time_(Giveon_EP))* and *[When It's All Said and Done](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/When_It%27s_All_Said_and_Done)*; the former was preceded by the single "[Heartbreak Anniversary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heartbreak_Anniversary)", which peaked at number 16 on the chart and received [quintuple platinum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_recording_certification) certification by the [Recording Industry Association of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America) (RIAA).

- **Christopher Brent Wood** (born September 19, 1995), known professionally as **Brent Faiyaz**, is an American singer, songwriter, rapper, and record producer. Faiyaz rose to prominence with his guest performance alongside [Shy Glizzy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shy_Glizzy) on [GoldLink](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GoldLink)'s 2016 single "[Crew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crew_(song))". The song peaked at number 45 on the Billboard Hot 100, received [octuple platinum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_recording_certification) certification by the [Recording Industry Association of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America) (RIAA), and was nominated for [Best Rap/Sung Performance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Best_Rap/Sung_Performance) at the 59th Annual Grammy Awards. The following year, Faiyaz released his debut studio album, *[Sonder Son](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonder_Son)* (2017). He is noted for his "sly vocals" and "atmospheric [slow jam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slow_jam) productions," along with occasional rapping.
